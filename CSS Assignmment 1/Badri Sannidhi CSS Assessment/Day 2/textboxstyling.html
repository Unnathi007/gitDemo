With Japanese anime Jujutsu Kaisen: 0’s Indian premier, anime fans talk about the film and how the community has evolved over the years.



After selling almost 50,000 tickets in pre-bookings on its first day, Japanese anime Jujutsu Kaisen: 0, the prequel to anime Jujutsu Kaisen, hit Indian theatres on June 30. It pooled an unexpected ₹1.80 Cr on its opening day in pre-sales and outflanked its Bollywood competitors Rashtra Kavach: Om and Rocketry: The Nambi Effect.



Jujutsu Kaisen: 0, which was released in India 10 months after its U.S. premier, was the result of multiple petitions by Indian anime fans, and this reflected in the atmosphere on the first day. Fans decked in anime merchandise showed up to theatres across Delhi, braving the monsoon storm outside.



Neel Oommen, 20, and Aryan Sangar, 18 told India Today that there was “not a chance'' that they’d miss the first show. Dev, a 19 year old added that he “walked through water right up to my knees” to get there.
